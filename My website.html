About us

We are students from Killara High School, Matthew and Jason. We are dedicated to exploring the rare and unique animals of Australia, especially those adorable and amazing creatures found across the great land. We would love to share some information and fun facts about them!

Animals in Australia

1. Koala

The koala is a tree-dwelling marsupial mammal endemic to Australia, notable for its grey fur, large ears, and a diet consisting mainly of eucalyptus leaves. Koalas sleep up to 22 hours a day! Despite their iconic status, they face threats from habitat loss, climate change, and illness, which require conservation efforts.

2. Red Kangaroo

3. Wombat

Wombats are sturdy, burrow-digging marsupials known for their short legs and cube-shaped poop! Despite their robust bodies and sharp claws, they are very friendly animals. However, they face threats due to habitat loss and human activity.

Fun fact: Wombats have cube-shaped poop!

4. Quokka

Quokkas are small marsupials that live in Western Australia and are known for their friendly nature and constant smile, which has made them quite popular among tourists. Unfortunately, they face extinction threats due to invasive species.

Fun fact: Touching a quokka is illegal and can result in a fine!

5. Dingo

The dingo is a wild canine that arrived in Australia thousands of years ago. Dingoes are highly adaptable, able to hunt both small and large prey. However, they face challenges due to human hunting and interbreeding with domestic dogs.

Fun fact: Dingoes are nimble escape artists with rotatable wrists and flexible joints!

6. Platypus

The platypus is one of only two egg-laying mammals in the world. With its duck-like bill, otter-like feet, and beaver-like tail, it hunts aquatic invertebrates and is known for its venomous male spurs.

Fun fact: Platypuses do not have teeth!

7. Sacred Ibis

The sacred ibis is an omnivorous bird, often found rummaging through human trash in urban areas. Its association with trash has made it one of the least liked birds in Australia.

8. Rainbow Finch

The rainbow finch is known for its beautiful song and bright colors. These small birds, mainly found in Northern Australia, are endangered due to habitat loss.

9. Tasmanian Devil

The Tasmanian devil, a fierce marsupial, is endangered due to a unique form of cancer that has devastated its population. Despite their small size, they are loud and aggressive carnivores.

10. Australian Sea Lion

Australian sea lions are native to Australia, living along rocky and sandy coastlines. Friendly and playful, they are endangered due to human activity and habitat loss.

Fun fact: Australian sea lions have been known to rescue swimmers in distress!
